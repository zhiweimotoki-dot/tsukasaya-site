RE300 JP 第6回 — 入居審査申込書、契約終了の7つの方法、特殊リース、不動産管理契約

📚 約13,068字 🎙 Patrick J. Hogarty 📅 2026年7月

RE300 JP 第6回 — 入居審査申込書、契約終了の7つの方法、特殊リース、不動産管理契約

Pat Hogerty教授はShow 6で、前回駆け足で終えたCARの住宅賃貸借契約書（全6ページ）の残りを振り返りつつ、今回は入居希望者の審査から賃貸借契約の終了方法、特殊目的リース（Sale-Leaseback、Lease with Option to Buy、Ground Lease、Graduated Lease、Gross Lease/Net Lease、Percentage Lease）、そしてプロパティマネジメント契約（Property Management Agreement）までをカバーします。

PatはShow 6の冒頭で「I spent quite a bit of time going over... almost every single one of the pages in that application and contract.」と述べ、CARの契約書を徹底的に理解することの重要性を改めて強調します。「What I emphasize to everybody is that contract is something that you always want to make sure you're using the most current contract when you're dealing with a client.」

1. 入居審査申込書（Application to Rent / Screening Fee）

Patは書類カメラでCAR作成の「Application to Rent / Screening Fee」フォームを示しながら、エージェントとしての実務を解説します。「Keep in mind, this is what we're talking about when people initially get ready to rent or lease a property.」広告や看板を見た借主希望者が連絡してくる——物件を見学した後、次のステップがこの入居審査申込書です。

申込書の基本構造

「This rental application is going to be gathering some specific personal information about them. And you're going to use that rental application to help you make a decision on whether or not you want to rent to this person.」

  • Tenant / Co-Tenant の選択：「Tenant means that you're the only occupant that's going to live there. Co-tenant means that you may be renting it to husband or wife.」Patはカレッジの友人同士で共同賃借するケースに触れ、「Once they sign the lease, they're both obligated on that lease. If your buddy doesn't pay the rent, you're still obligated to pay the rent.」——共同借主は連帯責任を負うことを理解させる必要があります。
  • 物件情報・賃料・入居予定日：住所またはユニット番号、月額賃料、入居予定日（Proposed Move-in Date）を記載。

本人確認と審査の核心

「In order for you to run these background checks, you're going to need something that's going to be able to identify them — like a Social Security Number or Driver's License.」

ここでPatが最も強調するのは、審査の本質は正直さ（Truthfulness）の検証だということです。「One thing that you should let the client know... if they don't put something down on here, it's probably going to show up on that report anyway. The concept here is — if they lie to you now, then the chances of them lying to you in the future are going to probably be pretty high.」

Patは自身の大家経験から重要な洞察を加えます。「I have rented to people before that have had financial issues to deal with and they've been excellent tenants and I've rented to them for years. But they've been up front. They told me up front, I've had a bankruptcy, I've had some kind of financial problem. And then you're going to make that decision. But if they're lying, then you know you're going to have a problem.」

過去の破産を正直に申告した借主は信頼できる——しかし嘘をつく借主は、これからも嘘をつき続ける——これがPatの大家哲学です。

申込書の主要項目

  • 個人情報：氏名、社会保障番号、運転免許証番号、電話番号（自宅・職場）、メールアドレス
  • ペット：「Do you have any pets?」大家がペット不可の方針なら、ここで明確にします。「The last time I had somebody that had pets, they ruined the carpet. The odor was terrible. The cat shed hair all over the place.」
  • 自動車情報：メーカー、モデル、ナンバープレート。特にアパートの駐車スペース管理のため。「You want to know how many cars they're going to need to park. If a car is parked there and you're not sure whether it belongs... security may be looking at a roster and saying that car is not supposed to be there — I'm going to have it towed away.」
  • 緊急連絡先（Emergency Contact）
  • 立退き歴（Unlawful Detainer）：「Has the applicant ever had an unlawful detainer? Have they ever been essentially evicted?」
  • 重罪歴（Felony Conviction）：「Has the applicant ever been convicted of or pleaded guilty to any kind of a felony?」
  • 退去要請歴：「Has the applicant ever been asked to move out of a residence for any reason?」
  • 居住歴（Residence History）：過去数年間の住所と大家の連絡先——前の大家に確認電話を入れるため。
  • 収入・雇用情報（Income / Employment）：「It stands to reason that if you're going to be renting to somebody, you want to be pretty well rest assured that they have the money to pay the monthly rent.」勤務先、勤続年数、月収。共借人の場合は両方の収入を合算することも。
  • 信用情報（Credit Information）：Macy'sやSearsの口座状況——あえて申告させることで、信用報告書（Credit Report）との整合性をチェックします。
  • 個人照会先（Personal References）：親族（父母、兄弟姉妹、叔父叔母）など。
  • 署名（Signature）：「You normally cannot run these checks without having their signature. You have to produce a document that says that you have been authorized to run this check because they're disclosing to you personal information.」

立ち退き（Eviction）の厳しい現実

Patは申込書審査の重要性を、立ち退きの困難さから説明します。「The hardest thing to do is to evict people. That is a really tough job to do. It doesn't happen easily.」Howe Avenue近くで出会ったある大家のケース——借主が家賃を払わず電話にも出ず、自力で立ち退き手続きをしようとして5〜6ヶ月間一銭も回収できなかった。Patは「You spend the time getting the right tenant in there in the first place.」——最初の審査に時間をかけることが、最も確実なリスク管理だと説きます。

2. 賃貸借契約終了の7つの方法

Patは教科書の該当ページを拡大しながら、賃貸借契約が終了する7つのシナリオを解説します。

① 期間満了（Expiration of the Term）

「The most common way that any of these agreements end.」——借主が期日通り家賃を払い、大家も満足、契約期間が自然に満了。これが最も平和的な終了方法です。

② 平穏享有の欠如（Lack of Quiet Possession）

「A tenant is entitled to quiet possession and enjoyment of the premises without interference.」借主には、干渉されることなく平穏に物件を使用する権利があります。「If they feel that you're harassing them... the landlord's always down here pestering and bugging me... that's a way that it can be terminated.」

Patは大家の立入りが認められる条件を明示します： 1. 緊急時（Emergency）：「Smoke coming out of the house, lights flickering, a tree fell down on the roof.」 2. 必要な修繕（Necessary Repairs）——ただし「With the permission of the tenant.」 3. 内見希望者への案内（Show to Prospective Tenants, Buyers, and Appraisers）：「The ideal situation... is to have the people move out in the morning and have the new tenants move in in the afternoon so you don't have any vacancy.」Patは古い借主が玄関から出ていくのと同時に新しい借主がガレージから荷物を搬入する——そんな理想的な入れ替わりを実現したこともあると語ります。ただし「If you're trying to show the property to a prospective tenant, you want the place to look as nice as it possibly can. If the tenant is mad at you, they're not going to worry about vacuuming it up.」 4. 放棄の確認（Abandonment）：「You knew when they moved in that they had a house full of furniture. And all of a sudden... a U-Haul truck in the middle of the night, and there's no furniture in the house.」 5. 裁判所命令（Court Order）

③ 居住性修繕の未実施（Repairs for Habitability）

Patは居住性（Habitability）の最低基準を詳述します。これはカリフォルニア民法（Civil Code）のセクションに基づきます。

大家の法的義務（Landlord's Legal Requirements）： 1. 防水（Waterproofing）：「Effective waterproofing of the roof, exterior walls, including unbroken windows and doors. If the roof is leaking, if the door is leaking, water is coming in — that house is not habitable.」さらに漏水によるカビ（Mold）発生のリスクも指摘。「Mold grows very quickly and there are a lot of people that have a lot of problems with mold.」 2. 配管・ガス設備（Plumbing and Gas Facilities）：「Maintained in good working order. The garbage disposal works, the dishwasher works, the toilets flush correctly.」Patはユーモアを交えて「I got to go jiggle the handle five times in order to stop it from running — that is not working correctly. That's an easy fix. It's laziness if they don't want to fix it.」 3. 給湯・給水（Hot and Cold Running Water）：「If you're not able to get enough hot water, what that means is that the water tank is probably old, has so much sediment in the bottom.」40ガロンタンクが実質20ガロン以下に——これはタンク交換で解決できます。 4. 暖房（Heating Facilities）：「Maintained in good working order.」Patは低所得者向け住宅で暖房故障により高齢者が死亡したケースに言及。 5. 衛生状態（Sanitary Conditions）：「Buildings and grounds kept clean, sanitary, free from accumulations of debris, filth, rubbish... rodents included.」ネズミは電気配線や配管ホースをかじり抜き、自動車の冷却水漏れや停電を引き起こす——Patは田舎の納屋や車庫での実害例を挙げます。 6. ゴミ収納容器（Adequate Number of Rubbage Receptacles）：収集日までにゴミ箱が溢れかえる状態が常態化していないこと。 7. 床・階段・手摺（Floors, Stairways, Railings）：「Maintained in good order. If you walk down the hallway, they should be safe, they should be well lit. You go to grab the banister and have it fall off — that's not a good idea.」

借主の義務（Tenant's Obligations）： - 専有部分を清潔かつ衛生的に保つ - ゴミを適切に処理する（窓から投げ捨てない——Patはニューヨークのアパートでゴミが山積みになった光景を回想） - 配管・電気・ガス設備を適切に使用し清潔に保つ - 来客による器物損壊の責任を負う - 居住用としてのみ使用する（リビングでオートバイ修理をしない）

④ 法的立退き（Eviction by Law）

「The people haven't paid their rent on time... you find out that they're actually using it to run a business... or selling drugs. You want to have them evicted.」Patは先述の「5〜6ヶ月かけても自力で立ち退かせられなかった大家」の話を再度強調し、「It can be a very long drawn out process.」と警告します。

⑤ 放棄（Surrender / Abandonment）

借主が自ら退去し、もはや居住する意思がないことが明らかな場合。「If the tenant all of a sudden one day you go over there and they leave, they abandon it... that terminates the lease.」

⑥ 契約違反（Breach of Conditions）

「The landlord said that they would always make sure that the tenant had hot water. And now the water heater is broken for three weeks and it still hasn't been repaired — that's a breach of contract.」借主側では、住宅を下宿屋（Boarding House）として使用する、無断で壁を取り壊して部屋を拡張するなどの違反が典型例です。

⑦ 建物の滅失（Destruction of Premises）

「If the place burns down, the contract's over.」火災、暴風、洪水などの自然的原因による建物の滅失。単なる窓ガラス破損は滅失とはみなされません。「If a tree fell on the roof and put a big hole in the roof, that could be considered destruction of premises. You have to kind of move the family out of there right now.」

3. 特殊目的リース（Special Purpose Leases）

Patは住宅賃貸を離れ、商業・特殊リースの類型を解説します。

① セール・アンド・リースバック（Sale and Leaseback）

Patの最も印象的な例——Home Depot。「You may very well go out... locate a site, have an architect draw the plans, get them approved, and build the building. Now say it's Home Depot — they get the building that they want, exactly designed the way that they want. But they have got a lot of money tied up in this building... so they sell the building to an investor. That investor will buy that building and then we will agree to lease the building back from them for a period of time — say five, ten, fifteen years.」

Win-Win構造：Home Depotは希望通りの建物を手に入れつつ資金を回収できる。投資家（年金基金やREITなど）は優良テナントの入った安定収益物件を取得できる。

② 購入オプション付きリース（Lease with Purchase Option）

「You come to Sacramento... you finally find this property... you say, I really like this house, but I'm not quite sure whether the company is going to keep me here long enough. So what I would like to do is rent the house from you, but I'd also like to have the opportunity within the next two years that I could buy the house.」

借主は毎月\$50〜\$100のオプション料を上乗せし、その追加支払分を将来の頭金（Down Payment）に充当する交渉も可能です。Patは「The advantage to the tenant... if the house goes up dramatically in value over the next two years, they still can buy it at the price they originally negotiated.」——住宅価格が\$300,000から\$350,000に上昇しても、2年前に取り決めた\$300,000で購入できる——これがオプションのレバレッジ効果です。

③ グラウンドリース（Ground Lease）

「A parcel of land... You see this sometimes in a downtown area. A dentist... notices that there's a space or a parking lot down the street. What he's going to do is go over to the landlord and ask them if he can rent that space to allow his patients to have a place to park.」更地（Raw Land）を駐車場として賃借する——地主にとっては遊休地から収入が生まれ、歯科医にとっては患者用駐車場を確保できる。

④ 段階賃料リース（Graduated Lease）

「You may have an office that you're going to rent. You're originally paying maybe a thousand dollars a month. After one year, maybe it's going to go to eleven or twelve hundred dollars a month.」大家が空室の多い市場で借主を呼び込むため、1年目だけ市場価格より安い\$1,000で貸し出し、2年目以降に\$1,200〜\$1,300へ段階的に引き上げる——CPI（消費者物価指数）やインフレ率に連動させる方式もあります。

⑤ グロスリース vs. ネットリース（Gross Lease vs. Net Lease）

Gross Lease：「We as the tenant pay the landlord a certain amount of money. When the landlord receives that, they pay the mortgage payment, the taxes, the insurance, all the repairs and maintenance. I as the tenant, the only obligation I have to worry about is my own utility bills and my rent.」住宅賃貸のほとんどはこのグロスリースです。

Net Lease / Triple Net Lease（トリプルネットリース）：商業用不動産で一般的。「You rent the property... but what you agree to is you'll pay the landlord a certain amount of money, but you will pay the property taxes, you'll pay the insurance, you'll take care of the repairs.」大家は家賃を受け取るだけで、維持管理の煩わしさから解放されます。

⑥ 売上歩合リース（Percentage Lease）

ショッピングモールやストリップモールなどの小売スペースで典型的。「You're going to charge them \$2,000 a month. But if your gross sales exceeds a certain amount, then the landlord is going to ask you to pay them a certain percentage above your base lease.」

Patはクリスマス商戦のアナロジーを使います。「Thanksgiving hits and all of a sudden the malls are very, very busy. They produce a lot of business. In fact, they say most of the income that those companies make are during that short period of time.」——モール運営者にとって、テナントの売上が伸びれば自分の取り分も増える——だからこそモール全体の集客イベント（アートショー、クラシックカー展示会など）に投資するインセンティブが生まれます。

4. プロパティマネジメント契約（Property Management Agreement）

Patは最後に、不動産管理を受託するエージェントのための管理契約書を解説します。

契約の基本構造

CAR作成のProperty Management Agreementは、所有者（Owner）がブローカー（Broker）を代理人に指名することから始まります。「The owner of the property is appointing the broker to represent them.」

ブローカーに付与される権限： - 物件の広告（Advertising） - 借主の発見・選定 - 賃貸借契約の締結（Rental and Leasing Agreement） - 必要に応じた契約終了・立退き手続き - 一定額（例：\$500）までの修繕費支出——「You may have the authority to spend up to say five hundred dollars to do repair work without getting the owner's authorization.」

報酬（Compensation）体系

Patは報酬条項を拡大表示しながら、典型的な報酬構造を説明します。 - テナント募集手数料：初月家賃の1/3〜1/2（例） - 毎月の管理手数料：徴収家賃の一定割合（例：月額家賃の8〜10%） - 追加業務：業者立会い、修繕監督などの時間単位報酬

「You want to know what you're going to be paid for. And the landlord needs to know that they don't get some kind of a strange bill and find out that old Pat was over there showing the contractor what to do and is going to send me a bill.」——報酬体系の透明性は、大家との信頼関係の基盤です。

大家の責任（Owner's Responsibilities）

HOAの規則書類提供、必要な書類の整備など。「They need to provide all the appropriate documentation — if there's homeowners associations, any rules and regulations.」

Patは講義をこう締めくくります。「One of the jobs that you're going to be in is the creation of contracts. Lease agreements, rental agreements, all kinds of agreements. If you don't know what you're doing, wait until the day comes and the judge is standing there and saying, Joe, on this date, did you make this contract? You're going to go, God, that stuff comes back to haunt me, doesn't it? Yes. Because you need to make sure you understand what's going on with the contract.」
