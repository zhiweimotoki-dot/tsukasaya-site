RE300 JP 第5回 — 貸主と借主（Landlord & Tenant）、リース契約の全条項

📚 約12,098字 🎙 Patrick J. Hogarty 📅 2026年7月

RE300 JP 第5回 — 貸主と借主（Landlord & Tenant）、リース契約の全条項

Pat Hogerty教授はShow 5の冒頭、なぜこのタイミングで「Landlord & Tenant（貸主と借主）」を学ぶのか、その理由を明確に語ります。「The reason why we're doing landlord and tenant is because... this is probably in most cases your first exposure into you as an individual getting involved in real estate.」つまり、多くの人にとって不動産との最初の接点は「買うこと」ではなく「借りること」だからです。親元を離れて初めてアパートを借りる——それがあなたの最初の不動産取引になるとPatは言います。

今回の講義では、Leasehold Estateの4類型、LeaseとRental Agreementの違い、契約書の最低要件、Statute of Frauds、さらにCalifornia Association of Realtors（CAR）の住宅賃貸借契約書の全条項を徹底的に紐解きます。

1. 基本用語 — LessorとLessee、LeaseとRental Agreement

Patは用語の整理から始めます。

  • Landlord / Lessor（貸主）：「The person that actually owns the property. Their name is on the title.」
  • Tenant / Lessee（借主）：「Sometimes we refer to them as the tenant. We can also talk about the lessee.」

Patは語尾のパターンにも注目させます。「When we hear these 'ors', it looks like they're associated with somebody that owns the property. And the 'ees' tend to be somebody that's going to buy it or lease it.」——Lessor（貸す側）、Lessee（借りる側）。Grantor（譲渡する側）、Grantee（譲渡される側）。この「or＝与える側」「ee＝受け取る側」のパターンは不動産法の基本です。

Lease（リース）とRental Agreement（賃貸契約）の決定的な違い

Patは明確に区別します。

Lease：「A lease is a contract for a set time. It has a definite beginning and a definite ending period of time. And unlike a rental agreement, a lease does not require a formal announcement from either the landlord or the tenant that the agreement is coming to the end.」——開始日と終了日が確定しており、期間満了時に「30日前に通知」などの手続きは不要。契約の定めた終了日が来れば、自動的に終了します。

Rental Agreement：「A rental agreement is something usually that we're doing on a monthly basis. But there are rental agreements that will go for less than a month.」——月極め（Month-to-Month）が最も一般的ですが、週極め（Week-to-Week）、日極めもあります。

PatはSacramentoの実例を挙げます。「You will find places that will rent rooms or apartments... and they will rent them maybe on a week-to-week basis. And the reason why... we have a lot of people that come to our town... somebody that's in the construction business. They're going to be in town for four, five, six months and at the end of that period of time they're going to leave.」橋梁建設のクレーンオペレーターが数ヶ月だけ滞在するようなケース——週極め賃貸はこうした一時滞在者にとって柔軟な選択肢です。

2. Leasehold Estateの4類型

Patは教科書の図を参照しながら、4つのレッサーホールド・エステート（Leasehold Estate＝賃借権）を解説します。

① Estate for Years（定期賃借権）

「That lease can go anywhere from a year to 99 years.」名称に「Years」とありますが、1年未満でも構いません。固定された期間を持つ賃借権です。Patは視野を広げるよう促します。「Think beyond a single family home or a condo or a townhouse.」

農業リース——農家が隣人の40〜200エーカーを借りて作付けする。オフィスリース——歯科医が5年間クリニックを借りる。地上権リース——Home Depotが30年間土地を借りて自社仕様の店舗を建設する。Patは言います。「What I'm going to do is I'll just go ahead and lease it. I'll lease it for 30 years. With the option to renew it. And what I'll actually do is go out and build the building.」

② Periodic Tenancy（期間賃借権／Periodic Estate）

「That period to period could be month to month, week to week, year to year.」月極めが典型例です。「There's an expectation that I'm going to pay the rent by the first of the month. If I don't pay the rent, the landlord can charge me a fee.」通常は書面による合意（Rental Agreement）で、自動更新されていきますが、終了するには適切な通知（Notice）が必要です。

③ Estate at Will（随意賃借権）

「We will continue that agreement as long as we both want to do that. It doesn't necessarily have to be in writing.」双方が望む限り継続する賃貸関係で、書面は必須ではありません。Patは実際の経験を語ります。「At the end of that period of time, they say, you know what, I need another couple months and maybe you don't actually even sit down and make out a formal agreement. You'll just say, oh, you need another month to rent? Go ahead and rent.」家の建築が遅れて「あと2ヶ月だけ」——気心の知れた大家なら口頭でOK、というケースです。ただしPatは「In the real world, you want to have a written agreement.」と釘を刺すのを忘れません。

④ Estate at Sufferance（黙認賃借権）

Patは「It sounds sort of terrible, but in reality it's not.」と前置きします。定義はこうです：「An estate at sufferance occurs when a lessee who has rightfully come into possession of the land retains possession after the expiration of his or her term.」——正当に賃借していた借主が、賃貸期間満了後も（大家の承諾なしに）居座り続けている状態。

Patの実例：借主が新居の建築遅延で「あと数週間だけ」と滞在を延長。大家が新しい借主を見つけていない場合、柔軟に対応することもあります。「The minute they send me the check, and I agree and deposit that check, then we go on a periodic tenancy of month to month.」——家賃を受け取った瞬間、Estate at SufferanceはPeriodic Tenancyに転換されます。

重要ポイント：「An estate at sufferance does not require notice of termination because the expiration of the lease is the automatic termination.」期間満了とともに自動終了するため、通知は不要です。

3. リース契約の最低要件（4要素）とStatute of Frauds

Patはリース契約の成立に最低限必要な4要素を挙げます。

  1. 期間（Duration / Length）：「So there's an expectation. Month to month — I'm going to rent at the beginning of the month, out at the end of the month. Lease agreement — rent the beginning of May, be out next year in May.」

  2. 賃料額（Amount of Rent）：「You can't say to somebody... we have a rental agreement and then just let them know at the end of the month and say, well, for this month I thought it was going to be $400.」

  3. 当事者の特定（Parties）：「Who's the lessee? Who's the lessor?」

  4. 物件の説明（Description of Property）：「We are not talking about a legal description here. We're talking about a common everyday address, like 123 Main Street.」

Statute of Frauds（詐欺防止法）

「Under the Statute of Frauds, any lease lasting more than one year from the date of the signing must be in writing.」1年超のリースは必ず書面で——これがStatute of Fraudsの要請です。Patは「The whole point of the Statute of Frauds is to try to prevent fraud.」と述べ、偽りの陈述（Misrepresentation）を防ぐために書面化が必須と説きます。

「California courts have held that in the event the lease is written, it must be signed by the lessor. It is not necessary for the lessee to sign — the payment of rent and possession of the property is sufficient acceptance.」——借主の署名は法的には必須ではありません。家賃を支払い、物件に入居したという行為自体が承諾（Acceptance）となるのです。

4. CAR住宅賃貸借契約書 — 全条項ウォークスルー

Patは書類カメラにCalifornia Association of Realtors（CAR）作成の「Residential Lease or Month-to-Month Rental Agreement」を映し出し、主要条項を解説します。この契約書は全6ページに及びます。

最新版を使う重要性

「What's important about this is the fact that you want to make darn sure that you are using a current legal document. The laws change. What CAR does is they have attorneys... six or eight of them... that take a look at all the various forms and documents... constantly.」法律は変わります。最新版を使わなければ、執行不能な条項に頼ることになりかねません。

物件と入居者の特定

「The landlord rents to the tenant and the tenant rents from the landlord. The real property and improvements described as... 123 Main Street, Sacramento, California. The premises are for the sole use and personal residence of the following persons.」——ここに入居者全員の名前を列挙します。「This is not a rooming house. It's not a place that you rent out to anybody that happens to come by.」

動産（Personal Property）の扱い

「Any personal property that may be included in the lease — refrigerator, washer, dryer, dishwasher, things that are not permanently attached.」Patの経験則：「If I'm renting an apartment, I usually will rent it with a refrigerator because it's fairly small and it's a pain in the neck to get it to fit. People that rent a house on the other hand... usually come with a lot of stuff — they've got washers, dryers, refrigerators, freezers, beds.」

Lease か Month-to-Month かの選択

契約書上でチェックボックスによりLeaseかMonth-to-Monthかを選択します。

賃料（Rent）

「The tenant agrees to pay whatever the rent per month.」支払い期日、支払い方法、送金先を明記。

事前契約書レビューの推奨

Patが最も強調する実務アドバイス：「I recommend that you... leave that lease agreement with them and say to them... go through here and make sure that you understand everything. If you don't understand anything, just highlight it or mark it and we'll talk about it.」——契約書を事前に渡し、借主がすべて理解していることを確認する。これが後日のトラブル防止の最善策です。

敷金（Security Deposit）— Patの最重要警告

「One thing with security deposits I really want to tell you is that you really need to take care of those dead serious.」

Patが説く入居時チェックリスト（Move-in Checklist）の徹底： 「When the tenant moves in... you should be walking around with that tenant and walking in every single solitary room and opening and closing every single solitary door, drawer.」設備の操作方法（オーブンのセルフクリーニング機能、ディスポーザーの使い方、照明スイッチの位置）も同時に説明します。「I not only show them, but I say, 'Here, I want you to turn them off.'」

「As you go in a room and there's a stain on the carpet, you want to annotate it. If there's a hole in the door or wall, you want to annotate all that stuff.」そして退去時には同じチェックリストで比較検証——これが敷金返還の客観的根拠となります。

通常損耗（Normal Wear and Tear）をめぐる大家と借主の認識ギャップはPatのユーモア溢れる実例で示されます。「Your definition: if you put brand new carpeting in, when they move out, it should be cleanable. Their definition: well, my son rode the bike through the house. Yes, there are some track marks in the carpet, but that's normal wear and tear!」

Patは退去時の敷金精算について：「You have to itemize all the things that you have done... I always recommend, and I think it's even law, that you have to produce the bills to show them where the money went.」——スタンレースチーマーのクリーニング代\$300、ペンキ屋のPat Hogertyに払った\$200……すべて領収書で証明する必要があります。

Patのドア修繕にまつわる笑い話：「They say, oh by the way, there's a hole in the door. And they say, no problem, I'll take care of that. What you think... go down to Home Depot, buy the door, buy the stain, sand it, stain it, make sure it matches. What they think... I'll take the door off, reverse the door hangers and put it back on. So now the hole doesn't face the bedroom, it faces the closet.」——「穴を直す」の解釈が大家と借主でこれだけ違う——まさに書面化の重要性を物語る逸話です。

駐車（Parking）と収納（Storage）

「You need to talk about parking so that they're aware of it... you will be the one that will get the phone call... and they'll get angry with it because you never told them.」特にHOA（Homeowners Association）のあるコミュニティでは路上駐車禁止などのルールがあり、事前説明を怠ると後日クレームの元になります。

光熱費（Utilities）

Patの基本原則：「Anything they have direct control and consume, they're going to pay for. Electricity, phone bill, cable TV. If they're renting a house, they're going to pay for their own garbage.」

しかし水道（Water）については例外も：「You may very well say, you know what, I'll pay for the water. Why? Because you spend a lot of money to have all the landscaping done and you don't want them to look at the bill and go, oh my God, \$100 for a water bill! John, don't turn the water on anymore!」——植栽への投資を守るため、大家が水道代を負担するケースは少なくありません。水道代を大家が負担する場合、下水道代（Sewer）も通常は大家持ちになります（下水道メーターがなく、水道使用量から推計されるため）。

メンテナンス（Maintenance）

「You need to always react to whatever the tenant calls you. The tenant calls and says, I got a problem — you need to either go over there or get somebody over there or acknowledge their problem right away. Because some of those things can turn into a nightmare.」Patはトイレの水漏れを放置した結果、床下の梁（Floor Beams）が腐朽し\$5,000の修繕費がかかった例を挙げ、「A \$15 fix versus a \$5,000 fix — use common sense」と警告します。

借主がDIY修理を申し出るケースについて：「I highly recommend that you make sure that they understand how to do the work.」そしてガレージドアオープナーの交換をDIYしようとした借主がシーリングファンの配線をショートさせ煙と火が出た例まで披露——「They don't understand that those wires have a significance.」

ペット（Pets）

Patの友人が購入したデュプレックス（Duplex）の体験談：「Goes to open the door and practically darn near knocks him straight on the ground. What had happened is that the people that had lived there... had had animals. Well, animals do something called go to the restroom.」動物の排泄物がカーペットとパッドに染み込み、ドアを開けた瞬間に強烈な臭気で倒れそうになった——ペット可物件のリスクを如実に示すエピソードです。猫アレルギーの問題にも言及し、「I cannot be around cat hair because my kids are allergic to it. Cats seem to be more of a problem than dogs.」と付け加えます。

改造・修繕（Alterations and Repairs）

「You do not under any circumstance want the tenant to ever do any kind of alterations or repairs without your authorization. Period.」— 一切例外なし。

鍵（Locks and Keys）／大家の立入り権

「Typically what you're going to find out is the landlord needs to have the permission of the tenant. You do not rent it out to somebody and then just think you can come and go as you please. They have the same expectation of the use of that property as if they owned it themselves.」

サブリース禁止（Assignment and Subletting）

「You cannot sublease it and you cannot assign it to somebody else without talking to me. Period. End of discussion.」

借家人保険（Renter's Insurance）

「I always make sure that the tenant understands that their belongings are not covered under my insurance... If the house burns down, if they don't have insurance, their property's gone.」Patは法的根拠も示します。「I have no insurable interest in their property. It's the same thing like me going up to you at the airport and saying, oh by the way, can I buy a life insurance policy on you in case the airplane crashes?」

ウォーターベッド（Water Beds）

「Water weighs about eight pounds per gallon. If you've got a hundred gallons that go in the water bed, that could be two, three, four hundred pounds. Your floor may or may not be able to handle that weight.」さらに漏水リスクも。

調停（Mediation）と仲裁（Arbitration）

Patは二つの違いを明確にします。「Mediation means we're going to bring somebody in that's going to be kind of like a marriage counselor to kind of help us work out our disputes. Arbitrator... is going to look at and hear both sides and finally say, you know what, landlord, you're wrong, tenant you're wrong, pay them. And their word's final.」

📊 2026年データ追記

  • AB 1482（Tenant Protection Act）：カリフォルニア州の年間家賃上昇上限は、2026年時点で「5%＋地域CPI（最大10%を上限）」が継続中。15年以上前建築の物件のほとんどが対象です。
  • 正当な退去理由（Just Cause Eviction）：AB 1482により、12ヶ月以上居住している借主に対しては「正当な理由」なく立ち退きを求められません（一部例外あり）。
  • 敷金上限：2024年7月より、カリフォルニア州では無家具物件の敷金を家賃1ヶ月分までに制限（AB 12）。物件オーナーが2軒以下の物件を所有する小規模大家など一部免除あり。
  • Sacramento市では、AB 1482に加えて市独自の家賃統制条例（Rent Stabilization Program）があり、年間上昇率はさらに厳しく制限されています。
  • 2026年のSacramento Metro Areaの平均月額家賃は1ベッドルーム約\$1,650、2ベッドルーム約\$2,000（Zillow調べ）。空室率は約4.2%と、依然として大家に有利な市場です。
